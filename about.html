Bio

I am a Mechanical Engineering student at Columbia University, minoring in Applied Physics. My work thrives at the intersection of condensed matter physics, and material synthesis instrumentation. I am driven by the challenge of bridging theoretical physics with rigorous mechanical execution, whether that means modeling the hydrodynamic flow of heat in extreme thermal conductors or engineering accessible microscale fabrication tools.

Recently, my R&D efforts have ranged from designing DC magnetron sputtering systems for open-source hacker fabs to developing cost-efficient photolithography machines aiming to democratize access tosemiconductor manufacturing. Across these domains, my goal is to translate complex physical phenomena into tangible, reproducible hardware.

Beyond the lab, as a Program Manager for Engineers Without Borders, I lead missions that apply engineering principles to real-world community challenges, specifically focusing on sustainable infrastructure in Morocco.
